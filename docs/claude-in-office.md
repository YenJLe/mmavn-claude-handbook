# Claude in Office

*Claude lives inside PowerPoint and Excel. Click the Claude icon in your toolbar to open it alongside your file. Already deployed org-wide.*

!!! tip "No Claude icon?"
    Insert → Get Add-ins → search "Claude by Anthropic".

## Claude in PowerPoint

*Already in your toolbar. Click the Claude icon to open.*

### What it does

- **Template-aware generation.** Reads your slide masters, layouts, fonts, and colors. Output matches your formatting automatically.
- **Full deck creation.** Describe what you need and Claude builds the complete slide structure with proper layouts.
- **Diagrams and charts.** Converts bullet lists into native PowerPoint charts and diagrams you can keep editing.
- **Cross-app workflow.** Pull data from an open Excel workbook directly into slides. No copy-paste.
- **Design and content editing in natural language.** "Standardize fonts across the deck" or "Make this slide less dense" and Claude handles it.
- **Persistent memory via custom instructions.** Set your brand colors, fonts, and slide rules once. Claude applies them to every session automatically.

### Try asking

- "Add a new slide summarizing our 2026 AI training roadmap."
- "Make this slide cleaner. Split into two slides if needed."
- "Create a visual diagram showing our event production process."
- "Create a chart slide using the data in my open workbook."
- "Standardize fonts and spacing across the deck."

!!! note "If it uses the wrong fonts"
    Make sure your deck has slide masters defined, and add custom instructions telling Claude which fonts and colors to use. You will still have to remind it sometimes.

## Claude in Excel

*AI that understands your entire workbook.*

### What you can do

- **Formula building.** Write, explain, and debug any formula.
- **Data analysis.** Trends, pivots, charts, variance analysis.
- **Data cleanup.** Standardize, deduplicate, split, and transform.
- **Cross-tab analysis.** Compare data across multiple sheets.
- **Bulk content generation.** Generate text for entire columns at once.
- **Large dataset summaries.** Analyze and summarize thousands of rows.

### Try asking

- "Write an Excel formula that does..."
- "Add a column that calculates YoY growth for each row."
- "Create a pivot table and charts summarizing Q1 sales by region."
- "Compare Q1 actuals on Revenue tab vs Budget tab, flag variances over 10%."
- "Clean up this contact list: fix phone numbers, capitalization, duplicates."
- "Split full names in column A into first name and last name columns."
- "Write a description of each program based on the program name."
- "Generate email subject lines for each event in column B."
- "Summarize the key trends in this 5,000-row dataset."

!!! tip "Notice this"
    Claude references specific cells and tabs in its answers. It is not guessing. If it ever cites a cell or range you did not expect, open that cell and check.
